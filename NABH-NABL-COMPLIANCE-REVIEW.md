# NABH & NABL Compliance Review — SphereHealth / BIMS HIS

> **Date:** 2026-07-12 · **Branch:** `claude/multi-hospital-deploy` · **Scope:** full backend + frontend codebase.
> **Standards:** NABH Hospital Accreditation Standards (5th ed.) — AAC, COP, MOM, PRE, HIC, PSQ/CQI, IMS/MRD, FMS/HRM; and NABL 112 / **ISO 15189:2022** for the medical laboratory.
> **Method:** 10 expert auditors (one per chapter/domain) mapped the code against each standard; an **adversarial verifier then re-checked every claimed gap** against the codebase to eliminate false positives. Every finding is grounded in `file:line` evidence. A register that exists only as a schema — never populated by a real clinical-event hook, or never surfaced to a surveyor — is scored **Partial**, not compliant.
> **Software-scope caveat:** an HIS can satisfy only the *record / workflow-enforcement / documentation* elements of NABH/NABL. Physical infrastructure, staffing, training delivery and committee *processes* are out of software scope (`N/A (non-software)`) — the hospital's responsibility, not a product defect.

## Domain detail

### AAC — Access, Assessment & Continuity of Care

**Readiness:** Strong / Mostly compliant (~85%). All AAC objective elements that software can satisfy are present and mostly enforced; the material gaps are (a) discharge-summary content-completeness is not enforced at finalize, (b) no admit-to-initial-assessment timeframe SLA indicator, and (c) a readmission-register diagnosis field reads a non-existent property so the diagnosis/same-diagnosis columns silently stay blank.

The AAC pathway is one of the better-built parts of this HIS. Registration/UHID is atomic and unique; a 5-level emergency triage is schema-enforced; initial assessment is split into signed Doctor + Nurse records with one-per-role uniqueness and a full screening battery (NRS-2002 nutrition, Barthel functional/ADL, Morse fall, Braden, pain); reassessment cadence is tracked by a dedicated compliance service with an OVERDUE sweep cron; care plans, bed-transfer/SBAR handover, referral letters, LAMA/DAMA attestation, discharge TAT and 30-day readmission auto-emit all exist and are surfaced to surveyors via printables. The finalize path for discharge summaries is well-gated (MCI reg no, death cause, PROM/PREM, nursing handover) and the finalized record is immutable. Weaknesses are enforcement-of-completeness (discharge content, IA timeframe) rather than absence, plus one data-quality defect in the readmission emitter.

### COP — Care of Patients

**Readiness:** Mostly compliant (~80%) — emergency, code-blue, nursing plans, pain, fall, pressure-ulcer, restraint, nutrition and mortality/mortuary are genuinely wired end-to-end; the one material hole is blood-transfusion bedside monitoring/reaction capture (schema exists, no write surface) and the absence of a dedicated vulnerable-patient identification workflow.

The COP register layer is unusually mature: a central nabhRegisterEmitter fans out from real clinical save-paths (nursing assessments, doctor orders, vital sheets, discharge/death notes, emergency visits) into append-only registers, and almost every register is both surfaced (NABHRegistersDashboard, dedicated pages) and printable for surveyors. Emergency/triage timing, Code-Blue response-time (MRT), nursing care plans (sign+lock), pain (auto reassessment window), fall (Morse), pressure ulcer (Braden, auto-sentinel on HAPU III+), restraint (order+consent+monitoring+removal), dietetics (assessment→therapeutic diet, FSSAI allergens) and mortality+mortuary (3-signature handover) are all implemented in code, not just modelled. The main gap: BloodTransfusionRegister has a comprehensive schema (cross-match, intra-transfusion Q15 vitals, 48h reaction window, ADR cross-link) and consent is verified against a signed ConsentForm, but the only endpoints are create-draft + list — there is no route/UI to record cross-match result, transfusion start/end, intra-transfusion monitoring, or a transfusion reaction, so the monitoring/reaction portions of COP.16/MOM.4 cannot be populated. Secondary: no dedicated identification/assessment workflow for vulnerable/high-risk cohorts (elderly, paediatric, obstetric) beyond fall/pressure/DVT scores.
